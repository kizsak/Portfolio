Pixel Horses

Digital image, 2026

The 100 Day Project

I am using the 2026 100 Day Project to work through a particular haze of grief. I live with a cocktail of ailments that have culminated in a debilitating case of long COVID. When COVID disabled me, I suffered a particular trauma at the hands of the people I loved most in the world, who stopped speaking to me--in unison--in 2023. I have written about this experience elsewhere–I describe the experience of watching long-standing friendships become brittle, develop cracks, then eventually implode. What remained after this moment were hundreds of photographs. They are casual photos, images documenting ordinary moments of friendship: shared meals, trips, celebrations, casual afternoons. The people who gave those moments meaning are still present in the photographs, but the relationships themselves no longer exist. In an effort to reclaim 20 years of memory, I am removing those friends from the photographs. Using digital and computational editing techniques—cloning, reconstruction, and other forms of image manipulation— I gradually erase their figures from the images. The environments remain: a room, a street, a table, a landscape. The architecture of the memory persists, but the people who once defined it disappear. The goal is not to pretend these friendships never existed. Instead, the process is about reclaiming the memories themselves. By editing the images, I create a new relationship to them—one that allows me to approach the places, moments, and fragments of those years without being overwhelmed by the loss embedded in the original photographs.
