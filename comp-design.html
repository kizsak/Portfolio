Simulated Polaroid

Digital image, 2026
I generated a synthetic photograph of an Ophrys apifera (Bee Orchid) using Midjourney. I then wrote a Python script (download) that algorithmically simulates material decay by introducing staged degradations—edge diffusion, tonal compression, color drift, and localized light artifacts. Rather than applying a single filter, the script executes these transformations sequentially, allowing each computational layer to interfere with and modify the previous one.

Simulated Daguerreotype

Digital image, 2025
Process notes: I used Midjourney to create a synthetic photograph of an Helenium autumnale (Sneezeweed) in bloom. I then processed the image using a Python script that converts the photograph to grayscale, reshapes its tonal curve, and maps it onto a simulated silver plate surface with sheen, vignette falloff, and tarnish artifacts.

Simulated 35mm Slide

Digital image, 2025
Process notes: I used Midjourney to create a synthetic photographic image of a field of Rudbeckia hirta (Black-Eyed Susans). I then processed the image with a Python script (download) that animates the gradual emergence of the image using layered noise fields, threshold masks, bloom effects, and evolving color casts that simulate photographic development in a chemical bath. Each frame reveals additional image detail until the photograph fully resolves.

Digital image, 2025
I created a large library (1,000+) individually generated mark-making elements created on transparent backgrounds. I used dozens of brush, smear, scrape, and texture variations as standalone PNG files. I then took a photograph I had taken of a sunset, and begin to digitally collage the marks into the frame, roughly using the strata and color palette created in the original photograph as the primary compositional elements in the finished piece. I digitally layered the marks I had made, arranging them in horizontal bands to establish a gradient shift from warm oranges and reds to cooler violets and blues. Rather than blending with a brush tool, I relied on opacity adjustments, layering order, and overlap to create transitions between color zones. The glow at the horizon emerges through density — more concentrated, brighter marks placed lower in the composition — while upper areas become increasingly sparse and cooler in tone. The final image is the result of cumulative layering, transparency control, and careful color sequencing across thousands of individual elements.

Fractured Self, 2025

Digital image, 2025
I began with a selfie. I ran it through an AI tool that identified the hex codes that were prominent in my image–basically, my personal color palette. Next, I mixed those hex codes with prompts focused on my favorite flowers and created 700 images of botanical photographs on Midjourney in my skin, hair, and eye colors. Then, I wrote a Python script to remove the background of all of the images, so they all became cut-out flowers. Then, I hand-placed them into a digital collage, using my original selfie as a reference.

Simulated Heat Map Self Portrait

Digital image, 2025
Process notes: I began with a photographic self-portrait. I used Photoshop to construct a depth map of the image by manually segmenting the image into spatial regions using selection tools and tonal adjustments. I then processed this depth map with a Python script (download) that reads pixel-level depth values and remaps them to a false-color heat visualization.

Sometimes I Fall Apart I

Digital image, 2025
Process notes: I began with a photographic self-portrait and increased tonal contrast to emphasize facial structure. Using Posterize and Gradient Map layers in Photoshop, I reduced the image to discrete tonal bands and remapped them to a high-contrast red, yellow, black, and cyan palette. The animated GIF was created in Photoshop’s Timeline by shifting tonal thresholds across frames, producing a flickering instability that reflects the work’s episodic theme.
