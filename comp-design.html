Simulated Polaroid

Digital image, 2026
I generated a synthetic photograph of an Ophrys apifera (Bee Orchid) using Midjourney and composited it into a Polaroid frame constructed in Photoshop. I then wrote a Python script (download) that algorithmically simulates material decay by introducing staged degradations—edge diffusion, tonal compression, color drift, and localized light artifacts. Rather than applying a single filter, the script executes these transformations sequentially, allowing each computational layer to interfere with and modify the previous one.

Simulated Daguerreotype

Digital image, 2025
Process notes: I used Midjourney to create a synthetic photograph of an Helenium autumnale (Sneezeweed) in bloom. I then processed the image using a Python script that converts the photograph to grayscale, reshapes its tonal curve, and maps it onto a simulated silver plate surface with sheen, vignette falloff, and tarnish artifacts.

Simulated Heat Map Self Portrait

Digital image, 2025
Process notes: I began with a photographic self-portrait. I used Photoshop to construct a depth map of the image by manually segmenting the image into spatial regions using selection tools and tonal adjustments. I then processed this depth map with a Python script (download) that reads pixel-level depth values and remaps them to a false-color heat visualization.

Simulated 35mm Slide

Digital image, 2025
Process notes: I used Midjourney to create a synthetic photographic image of a field of Rudbeckia hirta (Black-Eyed Susans). I then processed the image with a Python script (download) that animates the gradual emergence of the image using layered noise fields, threshold masks, bloom effects, and evolving color casts that simulate photographic development in a chemical bath. Each frame reveals additional image detail until the photograph fully resolves.

Sometimes I Fall Apart I

Digital image, 2025
Process notes: I began with a photographic self-portrait and increased tonal contrast to emphasize facial structure. Using Posterize and Gradient Map layers in Photoshop, I reduced the image to discrete tonal bands and remapped them to a high-contrast red, yellow, black, and cyan palette. The animated GIF was created in Photoshop’s Timeline by shifting tonal thresholds across frames, producing a flickering instability that reflects the work’s episodic theme.
