Episodic Disability

Digital image, 2026
Process notes: Episodic Disability is a digital project in which I explore the nature of chronic illness. Beginning with a simple selfie, I increased the original image’s tonal contrast and remapped its color bands into a highly saturated palette of red, yellow, black and cyan. I then animated the image, shifting tonal thresholds and color mappings across frames. These subtle adjustments cause the portrait to flicker and destabilize. Facial features alternately sharpen and dissolve; shadows migrate across the skin; the image appears to pulse between wholeness and fragmentation. The portrait never fully resolves into a stable representation, reflecting my lived experience of long COVID.

The Measure of a Disease

Digital image, 2026
Process notes: This piece began as a reconstruction of Leonardo da Vinci’s Vitruvian Man—a system built on idealized male proportion and geometric order. I worked with Midjourney to create a drawing of the Vitruvian Man, then asked it to do a drawing of my face in the style of the original Rennaissance drawing. I used Photoshop to insert my head and adjust the figure's limbs to more female proportions. Next, I hand-drew layer masks, frame-by-frame, to preserve a tactile, hand-intervened quality. The motion is minimal but perceptible: the body flickers between historical archetype and contemporary insertion. Conceptually, the work is intended to capture the experience of Long COVID--new body parts failing practically daily.

Floriselanami

Visual Model, 2026
To scope out an assignment for a class (INST732 Entertainment Theory, College of Information, University of Maryland), I trained my own visual model. I created a dataset from the many historical “flower languages”-- they come primarily from England, Japan, China, and North and Central American indigenous nations. Many flowers have very distinct and specific meanings, so I hand-coded the meanings into 15 buckets, which transferred over to the model’s classes. I then generated over 500 botanical photographs on Midjourney, and I fed these into the model with their associated classes. In the final product, you can upload a flower image to the model, and the model will tell you what the "likely" meaning of the flower is--just in fun, of course. You can try the model here or you can watch the gif I made about it above.

The Dissolution of Self

Interactive Procedural Abstraction System, 2026
This project explores abstraction as a procedural system. Instead of describing what I wanted an image to look like, I issued action-based instructions that progressively transformed a single photograph through anywhere from 6 to 24 structured rounds of abstraction. It is a move from descriptive prompting to procedural prompting. Each round isolates a specific abstraction strategy and treats it as a system parameter. These include geometric control, shape translation, subtraction, structural emphasis, color reassignment, and rhythm patterning, among others. The work is structured and presented in Notion as an immersive, sequential experience rather than a static assignment. Each round unfolds step-by-step, guiding the viewer through the logic of transformation. Prompts, outputs, and reflections are layered vertically to create a sense of progression — almost like moving through rooms in a conceptual gallery. Notion becomes more than a documentation tool; it functions as an interface for systems thinking, where constraint, iteration, and accumulation are made visible.
